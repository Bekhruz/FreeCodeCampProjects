Mustafa Kemal ATATÜRK

Founder Of The Turkish Republic And The First President Of The Republic

Mustafa Kemal Ataturk was a revolutionary who helped establish the Republic of Turkey. He was Turkey's first president, and his reforms modernized the country.

EVENTS IN ATATURK'S LIFE IN CHRONOLOGICAL ORDER

  • 1881 Mustafa born in Salonika (Thessaloniki).
  • 1893 Mustafa enters the Military Preparatory School in Salonika and is given the second name "Kemal" by his teacher.
  • 1895 Mustafa Kemal enters the Military High School at Manastir.
  • 1899 Mustafa Kemal enters the infantry class of the Military Academy in Istanbul.
  • 1902 Mustafa Kemal graduates from the Military Academy and goes on to the General Staff College.
  • 1905 Mustafa Kemal graduates from the General Staff College with the rank of Staff Captain and is posted to the Fifth Army, based in Damascus.
  • 1906 Mustafa Kemal and his friends from the secret society "Fatherland and Freedom" in Damascus.
  • 1907 Mustafa Kemal transferred to Third Army and goes to Salonika.
  • 1911 Mustafa Kemal transferred to General Staff in Istanbul.
  • 1912 Mustafa Kemal successfully leads the Tobruk offensive in Libya.
  • 1912 Mustafa Kemal appointed Director of Operations, Mediterranean Straits Special Forces.
  • 1913 Mustafa Kemal appointed Military Attaché in Sofia.
  • 1915 Mustafa Kemal appointed Commander of Anafartalar Group.
  • 1916 Mustafa Kemal promoted to Brigadier-General.
  • 1918 Mustafa Kemal becomes Commander of Lightning Group of Armies.
  • 1919 Mustafa Kemal appointed Inspector of 9th Army based in Erzurum with wide powers.
  • 1920 Mustafa Kemal opens the Turkish Grand National Assembly in Ankara.
  • 1921 Mustafa Kemal appointed Commander-in-Chief of the Army by the Grand National Assembly.
  • 1921 The Grand National Assembly gives Mustafa Kemal the rank of Marshal and the title Gazi.
  • 1922 Gazi Mustafa Kemal Pasha wins the battle of Dumlupinar.
  • 1923 Proclamation of the Turkish Republic and Gazi Mustafa Kemal is elected as the first President.
  • 1928 Gazi Mustafa Kemal speaks at Sarayburnu on the new Turkish Alphabet.
  • 1931 Gazi Mustafa Kemal founds the Turkish Historical Society.
  • 1932 Gazi Mustafa Kemal founds the Turkish Linguistic Society.
  • 1934 The Grand National Assembly passes a law granting Gazi Mustafa Kemal the surname "Atatürk".
  • 1938 Atatürk dies at 09:05 in Dolmabahce Palace, Istanbul
